Physical Security

The Control System operates within the bounds of the physical perimeter of the user’s operational environment. It is important to maintain adequate security to physical aspects of the facility such as (but not limited to):

  • Fences

  • Locked doors and gates

  • Locked cabinets for devices such as (but not limited to); controllers, switches, firewalls, and other network devices

  • Communication cables; in particular exterior cables running between plant buildings

  • GPS antennas; provide incoming signals to network time servers

  • Access controls

  • Entry badges

  • Entry logs

  • Security personnel

  • Security cameras

These measures form the basis for the rest of the security operation of the system.

Users must take appropriate measures based on the business needs to protect the Control System operational environment including the protection of workstations, servers, and networking hardware.

You should also sanitize and authorize any USB drives and other external media before using them in the Control System environment. Many plants establish policies that preclude the use of USB devices and strictly limit other media uses.
