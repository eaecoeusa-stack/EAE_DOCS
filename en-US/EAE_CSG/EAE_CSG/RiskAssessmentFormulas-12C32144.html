Risk Assessment Formulas

Risk combines the extent of potential harm to an operation and the likelihood that the harm will occur. It is an omnipresent aspect of any operation.

Per IEC62444-2-1 requirement 6.3.1.1, asset owners must ensure that IACS cybersecurity risks are:

  • Identified

  • Documented

  • Assessed

  • Mitigated or managed

Risk cannot be managed without first being assessed.

Risk can be roughly quantified:

  1. Apply a scale to the level of impact.

    NOTE: For an assessment scale for impact, see the Appendix H of NIST SP 800-30 Guide for Conducting Risk Assessments.

  2. Apply a scale to the likelihood of occurrence.

    NOTE: For an assessment scale for likelihood of occurrence, see the Appendix G of NIST SP 800-30 Guide for Conducting Risk Assessments.

  3. Then take the product of the two to have the risk roughly quantified.

NOTE: The related document, NIST SP 800-30 Guide for Conducting Risk Assessments, contains detailed instructions and templates for conducting a risk assessment.
NOTE: The related document, NIST SP 800-39 Managing Information Security Risk, contains detailed instructions for managing risk.

Conducting risk assessments includes these specific tasks:

  • Identify threat sources that are relevant to organizations

  • Identify threat events that could be produced by those sources

  • Identify vulnerabilities within organizations that could be exploited by threat sources through specific threat events and the predisposing conditions that could affect successful exploitation

  • Determine the likelihood that the identified threat sources would initiate specific threat events and the likelihood that the threat events would be successful

  • Determine the adverse impacts to organizational operations and assets, individuals, other organizations, and the Nation resulting from the exploitation of vulnerabilities by threat sources (through specific threat events)

  • Determine information security risks as a combination of likelihood of threat exploitation of vulnerabilities and the impact of such exploitation, including any uncertainties associated with the risk determinations
