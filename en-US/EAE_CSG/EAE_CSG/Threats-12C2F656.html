Threats

A threat is any circumstance or event with the potential to adversely affect:

  • Organizational operations (including mission, functions, image, or reputation)

  • Organizational assets

  • Individuals

  • Other organizations

  • The nation through an information system

A threat affects via:

  • Unauthorized access

  • Destruction

  • Disclosure

  • Modification of information

  • Denial of service

Threats have some intent or method to exploit vulnerabilities in the Control System through either intentional or unintentional means. This intent or method is referred to as the threat source.

The threat sources are described in two of the related online resources:

  • ATT&CK for ICS Knowledge Base: It is a knowledge base useful for describing the actions an adversary might take while operating within an ICS network. The knowledge base can be used to better characterize and describe post-compromise adversary behavior.

  • MITRE ATT&CK for ICS Matrix: It is an overview of the tactics and techniques described in the ATT&CK for ICS knowledge base.

NOTE: For more information, see the two following related documents:

  • NIST SP 800-82 Guide to Industrial Control Systems (ICS) Security

  • National Institute of Standards and Technology’s Computer Security Resource Center

This Cybersecurity guide provides guidance to mitigate the threats by configuring in a securing manner and adding appropriate countermeasures.

Refer to the Assets topic for the list of assets considered critical to this architecture. If additional assets or network traffic flows between devices are included in your system, you need to consider these in your system threat modeling and take appropriate countermeasures.
