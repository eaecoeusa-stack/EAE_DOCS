Cybersecurity Best Practices

We strongly recommend following industry cybersecurity best practices:

  • Locate control and safety system networks and remote devices behind firewalls and isolate them from the enterprise network.

  • Install physical controls so no unauthorized personnel can access your industrial control and safety systems, components, peripheral equipment, and networks.

  • Scan all methods of mobile data exchange with the isolated network, such as CDs and USB drives, before use in the terminals or nodes connected to these networks.

  • Never allow laptops that were connected to any other network besides the intended network to connect to the safety or control networks without proper sanitation.

  • Minimize network exposure for all control system devices and systems, and ensure that they are never accessible directly from the Internet.

  • When remote access is required, use secure methods, such as a Virtual Private Network (VPN). Recognize that VPNs might have vulnerabilities and should therefore be updated to the most current version available. Also recognize that VPNs are only as secure as the connected devices.

  • Always disable ports of the switches which are unused.

  • Always keep the inventory of your control system like workstations, servers, switches and so forth, and monitor them routinely.

  • Confirm the cybersecurity features are always enabled.

  • Always deploy safety systems on isolated networks.

  • Secure all engineering workstations and never connect to any other network.

  • Review and assess your site’s cyber preparedness. Schneider Electric is a proponent of the NIST Cybersecurity Framework and is ready to assist.

  • The Schneider Electric Product Security Office continues to work with ICS-CERT and will update this advisory as more information becomes available.

  • Always refer to https://www.se.com/ww/en/work/support/cybersecurity/ for the latest list of security recommendations, or contact your local representations.

Schneider Electric works cooperatively with researchers, Cyber Emergency Response Teams (CERTs), and asset owners to address cybersecurity vulnerabilities that could affecting Schneider Electric products, software, systems, and infrastructure. Schneider Electric goal is then to provide accurate information in a timely fashion to help protect the company, Schneider Electric customers, and partners.

NOTE: If you find a vulnerability, please report it here: https://www.se.com/ww/en/work/support/cybersecurity/report-a-vulnerability.jsp
