Forced Variables Impact on Action Commands

EcoStruxure Automation Expert - Buildtime detects situations where the use of forced variables may pose potential risks and asks you how to react to these situations when they arise. These situations can occur when you:

  • Login

  • Logout

  • Change the Active Network Profile

  • Close the application

Login

Before login, forced variables may be enabled both inside the project opened in EcoStruxure Automation Expert - Buildtime and inside the devices themselves. In this situation, you are prompted to decide which forced variable shall be kept after the login. The options are:

  • Update Project: replacing the list of variables forced in the open project by the ones from the devices. The forced variables in the devices remain unchanged.

  • Update Devices: replacing the variables forced in the devices by the ones defined in the open project. This has a direct impact on devices operation as enabled forced variables will be created or deleted in the devices.

  • Cancel Login: in order to examine the situation before taking any decision.

 WARNING
UNINTENDED EQUIPMENT OPERATION
Before login, make sure that modifying the list of enabled forced variables in the devices will not have unwanted effects on equipment operation.
Failure to follow these instructions can result in death, serious injury, or equipment damage.

Logout

Forced variables that are enabled in devices do remain active after a logout. In this situation you are prompted to confirm the logout.

NOTE:

  • To regain control over forced variables and disable them, it is necessary to again login to the devices.

  • The forced variables are not persisted after a device reboot: a login is required to apply forced variables. The options are

    • Confirm the Logout: and leave the forced variables enabled in the devices.

    • Cancel the logout: in order to examine the situation before taking any decision.

 WARNING
UNINTENDED EQUIPMENT OPERATION
Before logout, verify that the following situations will not cause unwanted effects on equipment operation:
  • Disabling devices’ forced variables at the moment of the logout.
  • Leaving devices with active forced variables after logout (a login is required to regain control over them).
Failure to follow these instructions can result in death, serious injury, or equipment damage.

Changing the Active Network Profile

The Active Network Profile defines whether EcoStruxure Automation Expert - Buildtime is connected to simulated devices – (Local Test) – or actual devices operating in real-time – (Default). Changing this profile triggers login and logout with the same potential impacts as with forced variables. In this situation you are prompted to confirm the command; the options are the same as for Login and Logout. For (Local Test) mode only, the messages can be suppressed (select Do not show this dialog again, or select Settings > General > Safety > Hide all safety dialog boxes for local test network profile.)

NOTE: If you intend to change the Active Network Profile setting after editing the configuration, for example after executing a Deploy > Clean command, you first need to compile the project to assign IP addresses and port numbers to each logical device before changing the Active Network Profile.
 WARNING
UNINTENDED EQUIPMENT OPERATION
Before changing the Active Network Profile, verify that the following situations will not cause unwanted effects on equipment operation:
  • Disabling devices’ forced variables at the moment of the logout.
  • Leaving devices with active forced variables after logout (a login is required to regain control over them).
Failure to follow these instructions can result in death, serious injury, or equipment damage.

Closing the Application

Closing EcoStruxure Automation Expert implies a logout and the user is prompted to confirm the command. The options are:

  • Confirm the close: and leave the forced variables enabled in the devices

  • Cancel the close: in order to examine the situation before taking any decision.

 WARNING
UNINTENDED EQUIPMENT OPERATION
Before Closing the application, verify that the following situations will not cause unwanted effects on equipment operation:
  • Disabling devices’ forced variables at the moment of the logout.
  • Leaving devices with active forced variables after logout (a login is required to regain control over them).
Failure to follow these instructions can result in death, serious injury, or equipment damage.
