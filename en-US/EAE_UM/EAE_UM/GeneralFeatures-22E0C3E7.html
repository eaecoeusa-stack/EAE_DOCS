2. General Features

Watch

In the case of watching the variables out of EcoStruxure Automation Expert Buildtime the watch window will display the value in different time zones:

LocalTime: the UTC time from the Device will be converted to the local time zone of the EcoStruxure Automation Expert Buildtime PC

RemoteTime: the UTC time from the Device will be converted to the local time zone of the Device

UTCTime: the UTC time from the Device will be displayed without conversion

If you are working with times in the Device itself, the functions are available in two different manners:

Constants

According to this behavior there are two different types of Constant declarations:

DT#2013-01-01-12:00:00.000

DATE_AND_TIME#2013-01-01-12:00:00.000

TOD#12:00:00.000

TIME_OF_DAY#12:00:00.000

This way you can define constants in local time. This means that the value will be converted to UTC time by the time zone of the Device at the startup of the Runtime.

HINT: Be careful by using summer time compensation and time variables. Some points in local time do not exist or exist twice in UTC time.

DT_UTC#2013-01-01-12:00:00.000

DATE_AND_TIME_UTC#2013-01-01-12:00:00.000

TOD_UTC#12:00:00.000

TIME_OF_DAY_UTC#12:00:00.000

This way you can define constants in UTC time. This means that the value will be directly stored in the variable.

Alarms and Archive

Timestamps according to Alarm or Archive functionality are time stamped in the controller by UTC time and will be converted at the time of displaying it (for example in the AlarmGrid of the HMI) into local time (depending on the settings).

HMI

Currently it is not possible to send or receive Time variables. You have to use conversions to REAL instead.

Interoperability

Sending time variables from one device to another is done in UTC time without any conversions.
