Discovering the Devices

To discover the devices in the architecture, perform the following actions:

  1. Connect the Engineering Workstation hosting EcoStruxure Automation Expert to the system.

  2. Discover the devices in the Discovery view.

  3. Locate physically the discovered devices.

Discover Devices in Discovery View

When the Engineering Workstation hosting EcoStruxure Automation Expert is connected to the system, you can discover devices using the Discovery view.

NOTE: If you use routers or firewalls between your engineering workstation using EcoStruxure Automation Expert and the controllers, then you must enable multicast on your firewall for the discover service to work. For more information about routers and firewalls, refer to the Network Considerations topic from the EcoStruxure Automation Expert - Application Design Guidelines.

To discover devices:

  1. On the toolbar of the Physical devices editor, click the Open/Close Discovery View icon to open the Discovery view.

  2. In the Discovery view, click the button next to the Start discovery icon , and select Manual mode or Auto mode.

  3. Click the Start discovery icon to start the device search.

    Result: After the discovery starts, the Start/Stop Discovery icon becomes animated, indicating that discovery is being performed.

  4. Optionally, click the Discovery Settings icon, then select IPV6 DPWS or IPV4 DPWS, or both, and click OK. Then click the Start discovery icon again to restart the discovery service.

  5. After all devices have been discovered, either:

    • Click the Stop discovery icon to end the discovery process.

    or

    • Wait for Discovery to stop.

      NOTE: The Discovery view is dynamically updated as devices are connected or removed, when the Auto mode is selected.

Based on the your IPVx DPWS selection, discovery starts on the subnet configured for the computer hosting EcoStruxure Automation Expert. Depending on the network and router configuration, devices from other subnets can also be discovered.

Using IPv4 or IPv6, EcoStruxure Automation Expert can discover devices that are located on a different subnet, if the following conditions are met:

  • The network is properly configured.

  • The devices have a consistent network configuration (for example, IP address, subnet mask, gateway).

NOTE: An Engineering Workstation must be connected to the local subnet in order to configure the IPv4 or IPv6 settings of the devices.

After the IPv4 or IPv6 settings of the device have been configured, the device becomes reachable in the physical view, across subnets for other actions, such as ping, diagnostics, and security. Then, if desired, the Engineering Workstation can be disconnected from the local subnet.

After the device is properly commissioned and configured, any runtime communication, such as Archive/History, HMI, and cross-communication links are available across the different subnets.

Locate physically the discovered devices

After devices are discovered, you can use the Locate command to physically identify them:

  1. Right-click a device in the physical view or the discovery view.

  2. Select Locate.

    Result: A signal is emitted by the discovered device (for example, a flashing LED), so the device can be visually identified. Refer to the hardware guide of the device for more information.

The availability of the  Locate command depends on where it is executed. If executed in the:

  • Discovery view: The command is always available on the Schneider Electric device.

  • Physical view: The configured device has access to this command, if the discovered device is reachable, and if it is the same type of device as the discovered device.
