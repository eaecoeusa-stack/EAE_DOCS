Behavioral Logics (Frame-5A) - Inside structure

The most compelling feature of the CAT is its internal structure. It presents a systematic approach designed to simplify the development of complex behaviors while maintaining efficient performance.

Before going further into details, check the follow image representing the overall structural framework of the behavior logic CAT:

This behavior logic CAT is following the same structure as the one used in its sub-system CAT. There is inside this CAT:

  • An initialization frame.

  • Interface frames for the functional input and output.

  • The representation of the field interface signals (here explicit).

  • The interface towards the Children.

  • An area for core logics.

  • A frame devoted to OT / IT interactions.

Let’s focus here on the guidelines for the core logic only as the other components are either understandable by themselves or reflect the ones from the higher level structure of the sub-system.

The logic area is categorized into three segments:

  • Frame 5.A Functional Behaviors; contains each of the identified behaviors. Each behavior is supported by a dedicated CAT. This structured approach facilitates maintenance and upgrades to the code.

  • Frame 5.B Coordination Logics; aligns the requests of multiple concurrent behaviors with the availability of children and signals within the sub-system. This structured approach enhances operational efficiency and facilitates effective interaction management within the system.

  • Frame 5.C Interlocks Management; serves as the management hub for both incoming and outgoing interlocks.

    NOTE: This frame is not mandatory, its use depends on the specific characteristics of the behavior logic of the sub-system.
