What’s New In Ten Minutes

EcoStruxure Automation Expert (EAE) 25.0 marks an important evolution in Schneider Electric’s software-defined automation vision. This release continues the shift from traditional, hardware-centric automation toward an open, asset-centric, and container-based architecture inspired by IT technologies. Version 25.0 focuses on tangible customer value in three priority areas:

  • plant availability for critical processes.

  • modular scalability of systems.

  • improved engineering efficiency and usability.

Increasing Plant Availability Through IT-Inspired Orchestration and Online Change

A central theme of EcoStruxure Automation Expert 25.0 is higher system availability, particularly for critical and continuous process industries. This release introduces the first step toward IT orchestration applied to the OT world, leveraging the containerized Soft dPAC runtime to dramatically reduce mean time to repair (MTTR).

Instead of traditional hardware-centric recovery procedures that can take hours and require specialized personnel, IT orchestration concepts enable recovery actions measured in minutes. This approach not only improves resilience but also reduces operational risk during maintenance events. While 25.0 represents an initial implementation, it establishes the technical foundation for future capabilities such as load balancing and online upgrades of simplex controllers—innovations that were not possible in traditional PLC-based automation systems.

In parallel, EcoStruxure Automation Expert 25.0 extends online change capabilities with support for Modbus TCP online changes. Although online change is a familiar concept in automation, its extension to communication layers is a critical milestone. Modifying communication links without stopping the system is essential for high-availability plants. This release marks an incremental but strategic advance toward a future where engineering changes—including protocols, networks, and I/O—can be performed without planned downtime

Modular Scalability Through Open Adapters and Asset-Centric Design

Customer feedback consistently highlights the challenge of upgrading large, monolithic automation systems. Version 25.0 directly addresses this concern through enhanced scalability enabled by the Open Adapter concept.

Open Adapters allow multiple EcoStruxure Automation Expert solutions to be seamlessly connected, while preserving clear system boundaries. This capability strongly reinforces the asset-centric approach of EAE and IEC 61499, enabling plants to be structured as a collection of modular, independently manageable assets—such as filtration, dosing, or distribution units in a water treatment facility.

By modularizing systems, customers gain unprecedented flexibility: different parts of a plant can evolve independently, even running different EAE versions, without requiring a full-plant upgrade. This directly responds to a common customer constraint—limited windows for system shutdown—and transforms incremental modernization from a constraint into a design principle.

Supporting Large and Data-Intensive Architectures

As industrial systems scale, so does the volume of operational and contextual data required by operators and enterprise systems. EcoStruxure Automation Expert 25.0 significantly expands system capacity with support for up to 40,000 OPC UA variables per Soft dPAC instance.

This enhancement is especially important for customers integrating EAE with enterprise-level platforms such as AVEVA System Platform. In asset-centric architectures, OPC UA becomes the backbone for digital continuity between control, HMI, and higher-level software. By increasing OPC UA scalability, EAE 25.0 enables larger, more complex plants to maintain transparency and operational insight without architectural compromises.

Improving Engineering Efficiency and Troubleshooting

Transitioning from traditional IEC 61131-3 environments to IEC 61499 introduces powerful benefits, but also new complexity. EcoStruxure Automation Expert 25.0 directly responds to this learning curve by enhancing debugging and observability.

New multi-view debugging capabilities allow users to observe and analyze system behavior across multiple assets and levels simultaneously. Engineers can now trace cause-and-effect relationships across different parts of the plant in real time, an essential capability for diagnosing complex control interactions in distributed, encapsulated systems.

Additionally, enriched and more contextual log messaging provides deeper visibility into runtime behavior, enabling faster and more precise troubleshooting. These improvements significantly reduce the time required to understand, diagnose, and resolve issues—directly addressing a key expectation from plant engineers and system integrators.

Advancing Collaboration With Enhanced Multi-User Engineering

Building on the initial multi-user engineering capabilities introduced in previous versions, EcoStruxure Automation Expert 25.0 delivers meaningful usability and robustness improvements based on customer feedback.

While still evolving, multi-user engineering reflects Schneider Electric’s commitment to agile development practices and collaborative workflows. Customers are encouraged to actively use these capabilities and provide feedback, as continuous refinements are planned to align with real-world engineering processes and team structures

Simplifying Adoption Through Task-Based Workflows and AI Assistance

Recognizing that industrial automation is a historically change-resistant industry, EcoStruxure Automation Expert 25.0 strengthens its focus on user adoption and experience.

The task-based user interface continues to evolve as a key enabler for guiding users through common automation workflows. Rather than forcing experienced engineers to abandon familiar mental models, the task-based approach creates an intuitive, structured path that lowers entry barriers while maintaining access to advanced capabilities. This release further positions task-based workflows as the foundation of a long-term “North Star” UI/UX vision for Automation Expert.

Complementing this, the integrated AI Copilot plays a critical role in reducing learning time and dependency on documentation. By providing contextual assistance directly within the engineering environment, Copilot helps both novice and experienced users navigate EAE more efficiently, accelerating productivity and confidence.

Strengthening the Hardware Ecosystem

EcoStruxure Automation Expert 25.0 is supported by new and enhanced hardware platforms designed to maximize the value of the containerized Soft dPAC runtime:

  • AP310 delivers a highly robust, industrial-grade controller for critical process environments, combining high reliability with the flexibility and openness of a software-defined architecture.

  • M590D, initially released for the Chinese market, introduces innovation at a competitive price point, including built-in redundancy mechanisms and advanced diagnostics, further extending the reach of software-defined automation.

In addition, Modicon Edge I/O continues to mature, with full native integration into Automation Expert engineering tools. The inclusion of key modules such as HART enables deeper diagnostics for process industries, while eliminating the need to switch between separate configuration tools—significantly improving user experience and engineering efficiency

A Foundation for What Comes Next

EcoStruxure Automation Expert 25.0 is not just a feature release; it is a foundational step toward the next generation of industrial automation. By combining IT orchestration principles, modular scalability, enhanced data handling, and a user-centric experience, this release addresses immediate customer expectations while preparing the platform for more advanced capabilities in future versions.

As customers continue their journey toward open, software-defined automation, version 25.0 delivers tangible value today—and sets the stage for what comes next.
