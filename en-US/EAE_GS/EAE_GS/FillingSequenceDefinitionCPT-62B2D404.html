Filling Sequence Definition

As described in the topic About the Whole Milk Storage, the instruments detailed in the chapter Setting-up the Solution allow for the reception of the whole milk and transfer to the TR1 tank.

This reception and filling process of the tank TR1 is done by controlling the instruments in a specific order.

NOTE:

The sequence is divided into several steps, each step is done one after the other. Each step features an action and a condition that must be true to move to the next step.

Below is the description of the filling process of the tank TR1:

Steps Action Condition
Start When the start of sequence is required and if the level of whole milk in the tank TR1 is not too high and the valve TR1V02 is not open.
Step 1 Take the ownership of the instruments used during the filling (TR1M01, TR1V01 and TR1V02) so they can be controlled during the sequence. 2 second delay after the last action is done.
Step 2 Turn ON the agitator and set its cycle time to: 15s ON and 5s OFF. Agitator is turned ON.
Step 3 Open the valve TR1V01. TR1V01 is opened.
Step 4 Open the valve TR1V02, which let’s the flow of whole milk enter the tank TR1. Expected level is reached.
Step 5 Close the valves TR1V01 and TR1V02. Valves TR1V01 and TR1V02 are closed.
Step 6 Turn OFF the agitator. Agitator is turned OFF.
Step 7 Remove the ownership of the instruments. 2 second delay after the last action is done.
NOTE:

The step Start doesn’t feature action because it is a waiting step. When the initial conditions are true and the the filling process is required to begin then the first step is activated.
