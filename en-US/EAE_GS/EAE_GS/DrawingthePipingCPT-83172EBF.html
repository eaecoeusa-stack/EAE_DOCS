Drawing the Piping

The first step in the development of the process canvas is to add all the facility elements related to the filling process of the tank TR1: the tank and the pipes representation.

These elements are the skeleton of the unit. They are the one representing on the HMI the flows of material available on the unit: these flows of material are defined by the P&ID.

NOTE:

These flows of material that will be represented in the canvas have been previously given by the P&ID shown in the topic Process Overview.

To have a clear and minimalist representation of the tank TR1, the two next rules shall be followed:

  • The pipes shall cross each other as fewer as possible.

  • Each pipe shall have as fewer segments as possible.

Now that these rules are known, let’s start to draw this schematic representation of the unit TR1.

To do all these representations, first open the WholeMilkTank canvas and select from the toolbar the Line tool.

 

With this tool, draw the first line that will be used for drawing the tank.

 

This way, draw the other lines needed for the tank.

 
The tank is now drawn. The piping shall hence be added following the two rules and what have been defined in the P&ID.

After checking the P&ID, it is possible to slightly modify the piping to make the two rules respected.

NOTE:

The modification of the piping is respecting the instruments settings and their default positions.

From the modified P&ID, it is easier to distinguish the two flow lines of whole milk to the Filling sequences of the tank TR1:

  • The pipe linking the raw milk reception to the TR1 tank (in red in the image below).

  • The pipe linking the TR1 tank to the pasteurizer (in dark blue in the image below).

 

From what is shown in the image above, use the Line tool to draw these two flow lines.

 

Finally, to know which line stands for which flow line of product, let’s add a text at the edge of each of them.

  1. Add two texts and go to their Properties pad.

  2. Change the text from Text to Raw Milk Reception and Pasteurizer.

  3. Change the font from the size 9 to 18.

Once the name is added, the piping of the unit TR1 is now completed.
