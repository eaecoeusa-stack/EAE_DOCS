About the 'Whole Milk Storage'

As seen previously from the P&ID, the Whole milk storage equipment consists of two tanks units: TR1 and TR2. Both are used to store the whole milk (raw material).

Whole milk from the Whole Milk Reception line is filled into TR1 and TR2. Based on the scheduled batch size, the desired quantity of Whole milk is discharged from TR1 and TR2 to the Pasteurizer.

The Whole Milk tank operation involves three flow lines:

‘Whole Milk Reception’ line This line contains the flow of whole milk. It is used to fill the tanks during the reception of whole milk and to discharge of the tanks during the scheduled batches to the pasteurizer.
‘Clean-in-Place’ (CIP) line This line is used for the cleaning sequence. Once the tank is emptied, it becomes dirty. So, before being able to add new raw product, the tank and the line have to be cleaned. A special chemical is used to clean the tank unit.
‘Water’ line This line is used for the flushing sequence. Fresh water is injected into the line to remove potential chemical residues left by the cleaning sequence before the storage of new raw milk.
NOTE:

In this Getting Started application, we will only focus on the filling aspect of the Whole Milk Reception line from the TR1 tank unit.

The instruments used for the filling process are:

Asset Name Description Location
TR1M01 Agitator used to mixe and achieve an homogeneous composition of the whole milk in the tank. In TR1
TR1V01 Valve used to isolate the tank from the Reception Raw Milk Line. Below TR1
TR1V02 Valve used to isolate the Reception Raw Milk Line from the arrival of raw material. In the Reception Raw Milk Line
TR1V03 Valve used to isolate the Reception Raw Milk Line to the pasteurizer. Upstream to the pasteurizer
TR1LSH01 Digital sensor used to indicate high level of material in the tank TR1. In TR1
TR1LSL01 Digital sensor used to indicate low level of material in the tank TR1. In TR1
TR1LT01 Analog sensor used to measure the level of material in the tank TR1. In TR1
NOTE:

The three sensors (Digital and Analog) are cyclically checked, and their values updated. The cycle time is 500ms.
