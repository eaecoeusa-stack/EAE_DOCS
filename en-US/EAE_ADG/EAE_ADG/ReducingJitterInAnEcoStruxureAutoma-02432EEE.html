Reducing Jitter in an EcoStruxure Automation Expert Application

Jitter is the measure of varying time fluctuations in the delivery of data and communication signals in an industrial automation control system. In EcoStruxure Automation Expert, jitter mainly applies to I/O signal delivery, but can also apply to the execution of program logic. Jitter can impact an industrial automation system at both the fieldbus and the application level, in the latter case by impacting Application Response Time (ART).

In severe cases, jitter can cause I/O to apply their fallback settings (for example, in the CRD controller), especially during online change.

Jitter Mitigation Overview

This chapter presents the following suggestions for mitigating jitter.

  • Use EcoStruxure Automation Expert to reduce jitter in critical parts of your application:

    • Use multiple (up to three) resources with configurable (three-level) priorities for Soft dPAC (non-HA), ATV dPAC, and M262 controllers.

    • Configure bus master phases so that their cycles do not simultaneously occur.

    • Disable the re-mapping of symlinks during online change.

  • Optimize the Soft dPAC Operating System:

    • During installation, use a specifically designed script to enhance real-time operations (for example by tuning kernel throttling, disabling unused services, and so forth).

    • Install no third-party software on the host device.

    • Deploy a single instance of Soft dPAC to the host device.

    • Design the field device network so there is no congestion on the path from Soft dPAC to the field devices. This primarily impacts the Modbus TCP and EtherNet/IP communication protocols.

  • Optimize your selection of controller:

    • Let your project needs determine the controller selection.

For a synopsis of the goals and actions you can take to achieve them, refer to the Goals and Actions matrix.
