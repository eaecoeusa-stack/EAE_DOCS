Multi-User Definition

Multi-User is an EcoStruxure Automation Expert functionality coming along with EcoStruxure Automation Expert Platform.

Multi-User allows several users to work on the same Distributed Control Application. They can design in a collaborative way, or decide to work locally in a independent way. They can also interact with the same devices.

There is a risk that concurrent access by Multi-User could result in unintended changes or device state. To better understand these risks and how to mitigate them refer to the Multi-User Risks Prevention topic. The actions of all users should then be coherent towards a common and shared goal for the Distributed Control Application.

To prevent those risks, Multi-User provides a zone feature helping to efficiently work in a Multi-User environment and avoid concurrent access to devices.

 WARNING
CONCURRENT ACCESS TO DEVICES
When allowing several users to perform simultaneous or sequential modifications to the application under execution in the devices, ensure that these modifications do not have adverse effects on the safety of the local and system level application execution.
Failure to follow these instructions can result in death, serious injury, or equipment damage.
NOTE: To know more about these Multi-User zone features, refer to the Multi-User Zone Feature topic.
